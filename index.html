Travel Information

We will be sharing practical travel information to help make your journey as smooth as possible.

Flying to Peru

Most international guests will arrive in Lima, Peru's main international airport. From there, a short domestic flight will bring you to Cusco, the gateway to the Sacred Valley.

Arriving in Cusco

Cusco sits at over 3,300 meters (11,000 feet) above sea level. Many travelers choose to head directly to the Sacred Valley after landing, where the lower altitude makes acclimatization easier.

Altitude & Acclimatization

Drink plenty of water, take it easy on your first day, and don't be surprised if you feel a little slower than usual. Most visitors adjust quickly and have no trouble enjoying their trip.

Explore Peru

For many of our guests, the wedding will be part of a larger adventure through Peru. We've gathered a few ideas to help you decide where to spend your time before or after the wedding.

Lima

Peru's capital is known for its incredible food scene, coastal views, museums, and lively neighborhoods.

Cusco

Explore Cusco City, the Sacred Valley, and Machu Picchu — the heart of Peru's Andean region and the setting for our wedding weekend.

Other Destinations

Arequipa, Huaraz, Ica, Puerto Maldonado, Piura, and Puno are all wonderful options if you want to discover more of Peru.
